This model represents a group of people, with Jocelyn and Hilary named specifically. Each person has exactly one spouse, also a person; and a set of people they have shaken hands with (shaken). We assume that each person's spouse is also a person.

The first rule of the shaking protocol dictates that a person cannot shake hands with themselves or their spouse. In more general terms, a person's set of handshakes does not include themselves or their spouse. 

The second rule of the shaking protocol indicates reciprocity: if one person has shaken hands with another person, then the other person must also have shaken hands with them. In other words, the shaken relationship is symmetric for any two people. 

The first section of the spouses rule states that any two different people either have a mutual spouse relationship, or no spouse relationship at all. That is, if one person's spouse is another, then the second person's spouse is the first. Furthermore, no person can be the spouse of another person's spouse. 

The second section of the spouse rule states constraints that are imposed on any person. Each person's spouse's spouse must be themselves, and each person cannot be their own spouse. 

Finally, in the puzzle rule, all people excluding Jocelyn are restricted such that no two different people can have shaken hands with the same number of other people - that is, the number of handshakes (the cardinality of the shaken set) must be different for each person.
